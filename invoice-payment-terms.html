Invoice payment terms explained

“Payment terms” tell the client exactly when payment is due. Getting them clear — and visible — is one of the simplest ways to get paid on time.

The common terms

TermMeans
Due on receiptPay as soon as the invoice arrives. Fastest, best for one-off or small jobs.
Net 7 / Net 15Pay within 7 or 15 days of the invoice date. A good default for freelancers.
Net 30Pay within 30 days. Common with larger companies; expect to wait the full month.
Net 60 / 90Longer terms some big clients require — push back if cash flow is tight.

Which should you use?

Shorter terms get you paid sooner, so default to Net 15 (or Due on receipt for small jobs) unless a client requires otherwise. If a big client insists on Net 30+, consider asking for a deposit up front or offering a small early-payment discount (e.g. “2% off if paid within 10 days”).

Make the terms work

  • State the term and the actual due date — “Net 15 (due July 1)” leaves no ambiguity.
  • Put the total and due date where they can't be missed.
  • Mention any late fee up front so it's not a surprise.
  • Send the invoice immediately — the clock usually starts on the invoice date.
